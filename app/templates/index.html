What is WSD you ask? you came to the right place

     In computational linguistics, word-sense disambiguation (WSD) is an open problem concerned with identifying which sense of a word is used in a sentence. The solution to this problem impacts other computer-related writing, such as discourse, improving relevance of search engines, anaphora resolution, coherence, and inference.

Here's an example because I know you didn't read the definition above!

Consider the sentences below:

  • I loved the bass sound in yesterday's concert.
  • He likes to eat grilled bass.

The word bass does not mean the same in both the sentences

In the first sentence, it is referring to a type of sound/frequency

In the secind sentence, bass mean a type of fish

This process is referred to as WORD SENSE DISAMBIGUATION
